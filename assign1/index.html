About Andrew

The Past

I was born in suburban southern New Jersey in 1989. I was an imaginative child and would always look to pull my friends and family into whatever adventure I had concocted at the time. The most memorable moment was when I transformed our house into a functional hotel complete with placards, room service, and direct phone access to the front desk which was manned by either me or my sister. I was also a talented swimmer throughout my childhood and adolescence; I continued with this through the end of High School on the varsity team. After graduating High School in 2007, I attended Duke University where I studied Civil and Environmental Engineering until graduating in 2011. I promptly moved into the workforce but decided that the Civil Engineering field wasn't suited to my aspirations. However, I still maintain strong interest in environmental topics.

The Present

I am presently working in Miami, FL at a major global cruise line as an IT analyst. The role combines aspects of project management, business requirements gathering and analysis, system architecture design, and digital product roadmap development. It is a versatile role that affords me great opportunities including the ability to travel around the globe. Since I work within the shipboard division of IT, I often find myself flying to far away places to work one of our many system deployments and upgrades.

In addition to my professional career, I'm also pursing a second bachelor's degree in Computer Science (I assume the majority reading this are as well). By doing so, I hope to augment my technical skills which I believe will help me in my career endeavors. Looking back, I probably should have majored in CS at Duke as the field is more aligned with my skills and interests. I'm glad to be finally pursuing that goal. Onward!

The Future

In the future, I see myself involved in some sort of entrepreneurial venture, although the specifics are still unclear for me. I'm always thinking about what could be that next viable "idea". I think the entrepreneurial route suits my independent mindset; in my view it is the only way to true independence. I soon hope to get involved in local start-ups to get a feel for working environments outside of the corporate world while still keeping my day job). Overall, I am living life to the fullest and trying to enjoy every day of it. You never know which will be your last!
